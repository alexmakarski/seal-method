# SEAL Domain Checklist: Tree Service Business

Use this checklist alongside `/seal-audit` when auditing a tree service or arborist business.

---

## Data Sources to Inventory

### Job Management / CRM (Jobber, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Arborgold, SingleOps, spreadsheets)
- [ ] Job history — completed jobs, last 12 months minimum (date, type, revenue, location)
- [ ] Estimate/proposal log — estimates given vs. jobs won (close rate)
- [ ] Average job value — by service type
- [ ] Customer database — total customers, repeat vs. one-time
- [ ] Cancellation/no-show log — jobs scheduled but not completed
- [ ] Scheduling data — jobs per day/week, crew utilization
- [ ] Outstanding estimates — proposals sent but not yet accepted or declined

### Accounting / Bookkeeping (QuickBooks, Xero, CPA reports)
- [ ] P&L — monthly, last 12 months minimum
- [ ] Revenue by service type — if categorized (removal, trimming, stump grinding, emergency, PHC)
- [ ] Labor cost — crew wages, workers comp, payroll taxes
- [ ] Equipment cost — truck payments, chipper, crane rental, fuel, maintenance
- [ ] Insurance cost — general liability, workers comp, commercial auto, umbrella
- [ ] Marketing spend — by channel if available
- [ ] Subcontractor costs — crane operators, stump grinders, disposal fees
- [ ] Materials/disposal — dump fees, firewood processing, mulch

### Marketing Data
- [ ] Google Business Profile insights — calls, direction requests, website clicks, reviews
- [ ] Google Ads / LSA data — if running paid
- [ ] Website analytics — traffic, form submissions, call clicks
- [ ] Call tracking data — if using CallRail, WhatConverts, etc.
- [ ] Lead source tracking — how customers say they found you (referral, Google, yard sign, Nextdoor, etc.)
- [ ] Social media — if actively posting (before/after photos, storm response)
- [ ] Direct mail / door hanger tracking — if used

### Operational
- [ ] Crew roster — roles, certifications (ISA, CDL), tenure, pay rates
- [ ] Equipment list — age, condition, payment status (owned vs. financed)
- [ ] Vehicle/trailer inventory — capacity, condition
- [ ] Service area map — where jobs are concentrated vs. where you're driving to
- [ ] Subcontractor relationships — who, for what, rates
- [ ] Seasonal patterns — monthly revenue distribution across the year

### Customer Experience
- [ ] Google review count and rating
- [ ] Other review platforms — Yelp, Angi, BBB, Facebook
- [ ] Review velocity — new reviews per month
- [ ] Complaint log — if maintained
- [ ] Repeat customer rate — customers who've used you more than once

### Safety / Compliance
- [ ] Insurance certificates — current, adequate limits for the work performed
- [ ] ISA certifications — how many crew members are certified arborists
- [ ] OSHA incident log — if maintained
- [ ] License/permit status — state and local requirements

## Metrics to Extract

### Financial Health
| Metric | What to Extract | Benchmark Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Annual revenue | Total collected revenue | Context-dependent |
| Gross profit margin | (Revenue - direct job costs) ÷ Revenue | 50-65% |
| Net profit margin | Net income ÷ Revenue | 10-20% |
| Labor cost ratio | Total labor (incl. WC, payroll taxes) ÷ Revenue | 25-35% |
| Equipment cost ratio | All equipment costs ÷ Revenue | 10-15% |
| Insurance cost ratio | All insurance ÷ Revenue | 5-10% |
| Marketing cost ratio | Marketing spend ÷ Revenue | 5-10% |
| Revenue per crew per day | Daily revenue ÷ Number of crews working | $2,000-5,000+ |
| Revenue per employee | Annual revenue ÷ Full-time employees | $100,000-200,000+ |

### Sales Pipeline
| Metric | What to Extract | Benchmark Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Estimates given per month | Count of proposals/bids sent | Context-dependent |
| Close rate | Jobs won ÷ Estimates given | 40-60% |
| Average job value | Total revenue ÷ Total jobs | Market-dependent |
| Average estimate value | Total estimated ÷ Total estimates | Context-dependent |
| Time from estimate to decision | Days between estimate and yes/no | Under 7 days ideal |
| Outstanding estimate value | Total $ of unsold proposals | Pipeline metric |
| Revenue per lead | Revenue ÷ Total leads (not just estimates) | Context-dependent |

### Job Mix
| Metric | What to Extract | Benchmark Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Revenue by service type | Removal, trimming, stump grinding, emergency, PHC, consulting | Context-dependent |
| Job count by service type | Volume distribution across services | Context-dependent |
| Average value by service type | Which services are highest-value | Context-dependent |
| Emergency/storm work % | Emergency revenue ÷ Total revenue | Varies — high % = weather-dependent risk |
| Recurring revenue % | PHC contracts, maintenance agreements ÷ Total | Higher is more stable |

### Efficiency
| Metric | What to Extract | Benchmark Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Jobs per crew per day | Average completed jobs per crew | 2-4 depending on job type |
| Crew utilization | Billable hours ÷ Available hours | 75-85% |
| Drive time ratio | Drive time ÷ Total work time | Under 25% |
| Estimate-to-job conversion time | Average days from estimate to completed job | Context-dependent |
| Seasonal revenue distribution | Monthly revenue as % of annual | Expect peaks in spring/fall |

### Marketing Efficiency
| Metric | What to Extract | Benchmark Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Cost per lead | Marketing spend ÷ Total leads | $50-200 depending on market |
| Cost per booked job | Marketing spend ÷ Jobs won | $100-400 depending on market |
| Customer acquisition cost | Total sales + marketing cost ÷ New customers | Context-dependent |
| Lifetime customer value | Average revenue per customer over relationship | Context-dependent |
| Google review count | Total reviews | 50+ to be competitive, 100+ is strong |
| Google review rating | Average star rating | 4.7+ |
| Review velocity | New reviews per month | 4+ |
| Lead source distribution | % of leads from each channel | Context-dependent |

## Contradictions to Check

### Financial
- **Reported profit vs. owner lifestyle** — Owner says business is profitable but is personally cash-strapped, or vice versa. Check owner draws/distributions vs. reported net income
- **Revenue growth but flat/declining profit** — Growing revenue with shrinking margins = cost problem. Identify which cost category grew faster than revenue
- **"We're always busy" vs. actual crew utilization** — Busy feeling doesn't mean billable hours are maximized. Check actual job count and revenue per available day
- **Quoted prices vs. actual collected** — Are jobs coming in at quoted price? Change orders, scope creep, and collections issues hide here
- **P&L categories vs. actual spend** — Tree service owners often miscategorize equipment repairs as "supplies" or dump fees as "subcontractors." Verify categories match reality

### Sales
- **Close rate claimed vs. documented** — "We close most of our estimates" vs. actual close rate from the system. Most owners overestimate by 15-20%
- **"We lose on price" vs. actual lost job data** — Is price actually the reason? Or is it follow-up speed, professionalism, scope of proposal? Do they actually track why they lose?
- **Estimate volume vs. lead volume** — Are all leads getting estimates? How many leads come in that never get an estimate scheduled? The leak may be before the proposal stage
- **"Referrals are our best source" vs. tracked data** — Same as dental — this is the default assumption when tracking doesn't exist

### Operational
- **Crew count vs. revenue capacity** — Do they have the crew to handle more work, or are they maxed? If maxed, is the bottleneck people, equipment, or scheduling?
- **Equipment investment vs. utilization** — Expensive equipment (crane, grapple truck) that sits idle most of the week = capital drag. How many days/month is each major asset deployed?
- **"We can't find good help" vs. compensation and conditions** — What are they paying? Is it competitive? What's the turnover rate? Are they losing people to competitors or to other trades?
- **Service area vs. job density** — Are they driving 45 minutes for a $500 job? Map revenue by zone vs. drive time

### Seasonality
- **Annual budget based on peak months** — Owner projects annual revenue based on their best months. Check actual seasonal distribution — many tree services do 60-70% of revenue in 6 months
- **"Winter is slow" vs. winter revenue data** — How slow? Is there winter work available (snow contracts, indoor sales/estimates, equipment maintenance) or is it truly dead?
- **Cash reserves vs. seasonal gap** — Is there enough cash to cover 2-3 slow months of overhead? If not, this is the real financial risk, not the P&L

## Common Claims Without Evidence

| Common Claim | What Data Would Verify It |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| "We close about 70% of our estimates" | Actual close rate from CRM/estimate log — most tree services close 35-50% |
| "We're the busiest we've ever been" | Revenue per crew per day, total job count — busy ≠ profitable |
| "We don't need marketing, it's all word of mouth" | Lead source tracking — and is new customer count growing, flat, or declining? |
| "Our prices are competitive" | Market rate comparison for common services — are they pricing for profit or pricing to stay busy? |
| "We can't raise prices, we'll lose jobs" | Test data — have they actually tried raising prices? What happened to close rate? Many tree services underprice by 20-30% |
| "We need another crew" | Current crew utilization — if existing crews aren't at 80%+ utilization, another crew won't help |
| "Insurance is killing us" | Insurance as % of revenue benchmarked against 5-10% — is it actually high or does it feel high? |
| "Our guys are good, they're just slow" | Production per crew per day measured against job scope — is it a crew problem or a scheduling/routing problem? |
| "We make good money on removals but trimming doesn't pay" | Profit margin by service type — removals have higher risk and equipment cost. Are those factored in? |
| "Storm work is great for us" | Emergency revenue vs. cost of readiness + opportunity cost of cancelled scheduled work |

## Service Type Profitability Analysis

When job-level data is available, analyze profitability by service type:

| Service Type | What to Extract | What to Watch For |
|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Tree removal | Revenue, crew hours, equipment used, disposal cost | High revenue but high risk/insurance exposure. Is hazard pricing adequate? |
| Tree trimming/pruning | Revenue, crew hours, frequency of repeat | Lower per-job but more predictable. Are maintenance contracts in place? |
| Stump grinding | Revenue, time per stump, equipment cost | High margin if owned equipment. Low margin if subcontracted. Which is it? |
| Emergency/storm work | Revenue, response time, premium charged | High margin but unpredictable. What % of annual revenue? Over 30% = weather dependency risk |
| Plant health care (PHC) | Revenue, recurring contract value, materials cost | Recurring revenue — highest business value. What % of customers are on PHC? |
| Consulting/assessment | Revenue, arborist time, liability | High margin, low volume. Requires ISA certification. Being offered? |
| Lot/land clearing | Revenue, equipment cost, disposal | Large jobs but often competitive bid. Margin vs. tree care work? |
| Firewood sales | Revenue, processing time, storage cost | Often negative margin when labor is fully loaded. Are they actually making money? |

**Key flags:**
- If removal is over 70% of revenue — business is project-dependent with no recurring base
- If no PHC or maintenance contracts — no recurring revenue, every month starts at zero
- If emergency work is over 30% — revenue is weather-dependent, hard to plan around
- If firewood is a meaningful line item — calculate fully loaded cost (labor, equipment, storage, delivery) vs. revenue. Often a money loser disguised as a side income

## What This Checklist Does NOT Cover

- Arboricultural best practices or ISA standards compliance
- Tree risk assessment methodology (TRAQ)
- Specific OSHA safety audit (though safety metrics are in scope as a business cost)
- Environmental/permitting compliance (varies by municipality)
- Detailed equipment mechanical assessments
- Workers compensation claims management (though WC cost is in scope)
