# SEAL Domain Checklist: Professional Services (Law, Accounting, Consulting, Advisory)

Use this checklist alongside `/seal-audit` when auditing a professional services firm — law firm, accounting practice, consulting firm, advisory firm, or similar expertise-based business where clients hire practitioners to solve specific problems.

---

## Data Sources to Inventory

### Financial / Accounting
- [ ] P&L — monthly, last 12 months minimum
- [ ] Revenue by service type — practice areas, engagement types
- [ ] Revenue by client — who pays what, concentration analysis
- [ ] Revenue by practitioner — who generates what
- [ ] Billing data — hours billed, effective rate, realization rate, collection rate
- [ ] Accounts receivable aging — who owes what and how old
- [ ] Fee structure — hourly, flat fee, retainer, contingency, hybrid
- [ ] Write-offs and write-downs — unbilled or discounted time
- [ ] Payroll / contractor detail — practitioners, support staff, costs
- [ ] Software/tool costs — practice management, research tools, compliance, marketing

### Client Portfolio
- [ ] Client roster — name, engagement type, start date, revenue, status
- [ ] Client acquisition source — referral, website, directory, advertising, speaking, content
- [ ] Client tenure — how long each client relationship has lasted
- [ ] Engagement types — one-time vs. recurring vs. project-based
- [ ] Client lifetime value — total revenue per client over relationship
- [ ] Repeat/returning client rate — % of clients who engage more than once
- [ ] Client satisfaction data — NPS, reviews, testimonials, complaints
- [ ] Referral generation — which clients refer new business

### Practice Operations
- [ ] Practitioner roster — credentials, specializations, capacity, billing rates
- [ ] Utilization rates — billable hours ÷ available hours per practitioner
- [ ] Case/matter management data — open matters, pipeline, turnaround times
- [ ] Intake process — how inquiries become clients (steps, timing, conversion rate)
- [ ] Capacity analysis — how much additional work could current staff absorb
- [ ] SOPs and documented processes — what's systematized vs. ad hoc
- [ ] Delegation patterns — what work stays with senior practitioners vs. gets delegated
- [ ] Technology stack — what tools, how integrated, where manual gaps exist

### Marketing / Acquisition
- [ ] Website analytics — traffic, sources, top pages, conversion events
- [ ] SEO data — rankings for key terms, organic traffic trend, content inventory
- [ ] Paid advertising — channels, spend, CPL, conversion rates
- [ ] Content marketing — blog, podcast, newsletter, social media (what, frequency, results)
- [ ] Directory listings — Avvo, Martindale, industry directories, Google Business Profile
- [ ] Review profile — Google reviews, industry-specific review sites, count and rating
- [ ] Referral network — formal or informal, tracked or untracked
- [ ] Speaking / events — conferences, webinars, local events, lead generation from these
- [ ] Email list — size, engagement, nurture sequences, conversion from list to client

### Competitive Landscape
- [ ] Direct competitors — who else does this in the same market/geography
- [ ] Competitor positioning — how they message, price, differentiate
- [ ] Market size indicators — total addressable market for the practice area
- [ ] Competitive advantages — what this firm does that others don't or can't

### Client Journey / Experience
- [ ] First contact to engagement — timeline, touchpoints, friction
- [ ] Communication cadence — how often clients hear from their practitioner
- [ ] Status updates — proactive or only when clients ask
- [ ] Deliverable quality — standardized templates or built from scratch each time
- [ ] Post-engagement follow-up — what happens after the matter closes
- [ ] Cross-sell / upsell process — how additional needs are identified and offered

## Metrics to Extract

### Financial Health
| Metric | What to Extract | Benchmark Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Annual revenue | Total firm revenue | Context-dependent |
| Revenue per practitioner | Revenue ÷ Practitioner FTE | $200K-500K+ depending on practice |
| Revenue per employee (all) | Revenue ÷ Total FTE | $150K-300K+ |
| Gross margin | (Revenue - direct delivery costs) ÷ Revenue | 50-70% |
| Net profit margin | Net income ÷ Revenue | 20-35% |
| Effective hourly rate | Collected revenue ÷ Total hours worked (not just billed) | Must exceed cost by 3x+ |
| Realization rate | Billed amount ÷ Standard rate × Hours | 85-95% |
| Collection rate | Collected ÷ Billed | 90-98% |
| Billing realization × collection | Combined rate (the real multiplier) | 80-90% |
| Average matter value | Revenue ÷ Number of matters/engagements | Context-dependent |

### Client Acquisition
| Metric | What to Extract | Benchmark Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| New clients per month | Raw count, trailing average | Context-dependent |
| Client acquisition cost | Total marketing + sales cost ÷ New clients | Must be < LTV/3 |
| Inquiry-to-client conversion rate | New clients ÷ Total inquiries | 20-40% |
| Average time to close | Days from first inquiry to signed engagement | Context-dependent |
| Cost per lead | Marketing spend ÷ Qualified inquiries | Context-dependent |
| Lead source distribution | % of clients from each channel | Diversified = healthy |
| Website conversion rate | Inquiries ÷ Website visitors | 2-5% for professional services |
| Referral rate | % of new clients from referrals | Context-dependent |

### Client Value
| Metric | What to Extract | Benchmark Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Average client lifetime value | Total revenue per client over relationship | 3x+ CAC |
| Repeat engagement rate | % of clients with 2+ engagements | Higher = healthier |
| Cross-sell rate | % of clients using multiple service lines | Context-dependent |
| Client retention (for recurring) | % of retainer clients renewing | 80%+ annual |
| Revenue concentration | Top client as % of revenue | Under 15% |
| Top 5 concentration | Top 5 clients as % of revenue | Under 35% |

### Operational Efficiency
| Metric | What to Extract | Benchmark Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Utilization rate | Billable hours ÷ Available hours | 60-80% for practitioners |
| Leverage ratio | Non-partner practitioners ÷ Partners/owners | Higher = more scalable |
| Support staff ratio | Support ÷ Practitioners | Context-dependent |
| Average turnaround time | Typical matter duration | Context-dependent |
| Capacity headroom | Additional matters current staff could handle | Context-dependent |

### Digital Presence
| Metric | What to Extract | Benchmark Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Monthly organic traffic | Unique visitors from organic search | Context-dependent |
| Organic traffic trend | Month-over-month growth/decline | Positive = healthy |
| Top keyword rankings | Position for practice-area terms | Page 1 for core terms |
| Google Business Profile metrics | Views, calls, direction requests | Context-dependent |
| Review count and rating | Total reviews, average rating | 4.5+ stars, 50+ reviews |
| Content inventory | Published pages, blog posts, resources | Context-dependent |

## Contradictions to Check

### Financial
- **"We're very profitable" vs. owner compensation** — Is the owner paying themselves a market salary, or is "profit" their unpaid labor? Add a fair salary and recalculate
- **High billing rate vs. low realization** — Billing $400/hour but writing off 30% means the real rate is $280. Check realization and collection rates
- **Revenue growth vs. margin trend** — Growing revenue by taking lower-value cases or clients erodes margin. Check average matter value trend
- **"Flat fee is more profitable" vs. actual hours** — Track actual hours on flat-fee engagements. Many firms discover they're making less per hour on flat fees than hourly billing
- **"We're diversified" vs. practice area concentration** — Offering 5 services where 1 generates 70% of revenue is concentration risk

### Client Acquisition
- **"We get most of our business from referrals" vs. tracking** — Without a referral tracking system, this is an assumption, not a fact. What % can actually be attributed?
- **"Our website brings in clients" vs. analytics** — Check actual conversion events. High traffic with no conversions means the site is an expensive brochure
- **"We don't need marketing" vs. growth rate** — If the firm isn't growing or is declining, the referral engine isn't sufficient regardless of what it used to produce
- **"We're well-known in our market" vs. search visibility** — Brand reputation and digital visibility are different. Potential clients Google before they call
- **Good reviews but no review system** — Reviews came organically. That means satisfied clients who don't leave reviews are invisible. What's the ask rate?

### Operations
- **"Our people are busy" vs. utilization data** — Busy with what? Billable work or admin, internal meetings, and inefficient processes?
- **Senior practitioners doing junior work** — The $400/hour partner is formatting documents and managing scheduling. Check time allocation by task type
- **"We can't take on more clients" vs. capacity analysis** — Is this a capacity problem or a delegation problem? Could junior staff or technology absorb tasks currently done by senior practitioners?
- **"Every matter is unique" vs. actual variation** — Many practices have 80% repeatable workflows with 20% customization. "Unique" often means "not systematized"
- **"Quality requires personal attention" vs. scalability** — The firm equates quality with the founder touching everything. This limits growth to the founder's hours

### Pricing
- **Hourly billing vs. value delivered** — A tax strategy that saves $200K billed at 10 hours × $400 = $4,000. The value gap is massive. Is there a value-based pricing opportunity?
- **"Our rates are competitive" vs. market data** — Competitive with whom? The low end or the specialists? Check positioning vs. pricing alignment
- **Discounting for "good clients"** vs. actual revenue impact** — Track the total revenue impact of discounts. "Just 10% off" across 50 matters adds up

## Common Claims Without Evidence

| Common Claim | What Data Would Verify It |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| "We're the best at [practice area]" | Win rate, outcomes data, benchmarking against competitors, client satisfaction scores |
| "Our clients love us" | NPS scores, review data, retention rates, referral rates |
| "We get all our business from referrals" | Source tracking data — attributed referrals ÷ total new clients |
| "We could grow if we wanted to" | Pipeline data, conversion rates, capacity analysis. Can you actually absorb growth? |
| "We compete on quality, not price" | Win/loss data on proposals. What do lost prospects actually say? |
| "We have a great reputation" | Google reviews, industry recognition, brand awareness data, search visibility |
| "Our website generates leads" | Google Analytics conversion tracking, form submissions, call tracking |
| "We need to hire to grow" | Utilization data, delegation audit, process efficiency analysis. Is headcount the real bottleneck? |
| "We're specialists" | Revenue breakdown — does specialist work dominate, or is generalist work paying the bills? |
| "Digital marketing doesn't work for our practice" | Has it been properly tested? What was spent, on what channels, with what targeting and measurement? |

## Practice Model Analysis

### Revenue Model
| Model | What to Check | Risk |
|-------|--------------|------|
| Hourly billing | Utilization-dependent; caps revenue at available hours | Revenue ceiling = hours × rate. No leverage |
| Flat fee per matter | Profitability depends on efficiency | Scope creep kills margin; must know your costs precisely |
| Monthly retainer | Predictable recurring revenue | Must deliver enough value to prevent cancellation |
| Contingency / success fee | Aligned with client outcome | Cash flow unpredictable; limited to practice areas where this applies |
| Subscription/membership access | Recurring, scalable | Requires volume; dilutes personal attention positioning |
| Productized service | Standardized delivery, scalable | Commoditization risk; must compete on efficiency |

### Structural Risks to Flag
- **Founder dependency** — If the founder is the primary rainmaker, lead practitioner, and manager, the business doesn't function without them
- **Key client dependency** — Any single client representing 20%+ of revenue is an existential risk
- **No intake system** — Inquiries handled ad hoc, no qualification criteria, no conversion tracking. Every lead gets the same treatment regardless of fit
- **Time-for-money trap** — Revenue directly tied to practitioner hours with no leverage mechanism (delegation, productization, technology, scale)
- **Knowledge hoarding** — Critical know-how lives in one person's head. No documentation, no training, no redundancy
- **Referral-only growth** — Works until it doesn't. No control over volume, timing, or quality of incoming leads
- **No digital presence** — In an era where clients Google before they call, invisibility online is a competitive disadvantage regardless of offline reputation
- **Practitioner as bottleneck** — Client experience degrades during busy periods because everything flows through too few people

## What This Checklist Does NOT Cover

- Specific practice area strategy (tax law, estate planning, corporate law, etc.)
- Legal compliance, ethics, or regulatory requirements
- Individual matter quality or outcome assessment
- Practitioner licensing, continuing education, or credentialing
- Detailed HR compliance (though staffing costs and utilization are in scope)
- Malpractice risk assessment
- Detailed technology platform evaluation (though tech stack is inventoried)
